Early Life

Nicolas Cage was born on January 7, 1964, in Long Beach, California. He is a member of the famous Coppola family and changed his last name to Cage to avoid nepotism in the film industry. Cage attended Beverly Hills High School before pursuing an acting career.

Career

Nicolas Cage made his acting debut with a minor role in the 1982 film "Fast Times at Ridgemont High." He went on to star in various films throughout the 1980s and 1990s, achieving critical acclaim for his performances in "Raising Arizona," "Moonstruck," and "Leaving Las Vegas," for which he won an Academy Award for Best Actor. Cage continued to captivate audiences with roles in films such as "Face/Off," "Con Air," "National Treasure," and "Adaptation."

Personal Life

Cage has been married multiple times and has two children. He is known for his philanthropic efforts, supporting various charities and causes, including Amnesty International and the Make-A-Wish Foundation. He is also an avid collector of comic books and rare artifacts.
